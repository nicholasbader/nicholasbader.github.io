Class 1: Solutions to Exercises

Section 1.1: Riemann's Habilitation

1.1.2

Provide a computation which shows that the (Euclidean) line element for \(\mathbb{R}^2 \backslash \{(0,0)\}\) in polar coordinates takes the form of \(ds^2 = dr^2 + r^2 d\theta^2\).

Solution:

As previously shown, the (Euclidean) line element, or Riemannian Metric, for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) in Cartesian coordinates takes the form \(ds^2 = dx^2 + dy^2\), and there exists a map from \(\mathbb{R}^2\) in Cartesian coordinates to \(\mathbb{R}^2 \backslash \{(0,0)\}\) in polar coordinates, which is defined parametrically as \(x = r \cos \theta\) and \(y = r \sin \theta\), for all \((x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \backslash \{(0,0)\}\). We proceed by computing \(dx\) and \(dy\) in terms of \(r, \theta, dr, d\theta\), then making a direct substitution into the line element for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) Cartesian coordinates. From our change of coordinates, we have \(x\) and \(y\) as functions of \(r\) and \(\theta\) and we apply the chain rule:

\[ dx = \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} dr + \frac{\partial x}{\partial \theta} d\theta \implies dx = \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left[ r \cos \theta \right] dr + \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left[ r \cos \theta \right] d\theta \]

\[ \therefore dx = \cos(\theta) dr - r \sin(\theta) d\theta \]

\[ dy = \frac{\partial y}{\partial r} dr + \frac{\partial y}{\partial \theta} d\theta \implies dy = \frac{\partial}{\partial r}\left[ r \sin \theta \right] dr + \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\left[ r \sin \theta \right] d\theta \]

\[ \therefore dy = \sin(\theta) dr + r \cos(\theta) d\theta. \]

Then, we make the following substitution and simplify:

\[ ds^2 = dx^2 + dy^2 = \left[\cos(\theta) dr - r \sin(\theta) d\theta\right]^2 + \left[\sin(\theta) dr + r \cos(\theta) d\theta\right]^2 \]

\[ = \left[\cos^2(\theta) dr^2 - 2r \cos(\theta) \sin(\theta) dr d\theta + r^2 \sin^2(\theta) d\theta^2 \right] + \left[\sin^2(\theta) dr^2 + 2r \cos(\theta) \sin(\theta) dr d\theta + r^2 \cos^2(\theta) d\theta^2 \right] \]

\[ = \left[\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta)\right] dr^2 + r^2 \left[\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta)\right] d\theta^2 \]

\[ = dr^2 + r^2 d\theta^2, \text{ as desired.} \]
